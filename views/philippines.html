~ / Brian Norlander / Travel / Philippines

I visited Manila, Davao City, Samal Island, Mindinau, Lakue Sebu, Iloilo, Guimaras, Mambukal, Hong Kong, and Corrigedor from Dec. 29th to January 12th 2018

Manila

Manila, the capitol and chief city of the Philippines, is the center of the country's economic, political, social, and cultural activity. It also has the most dense city proper in the world.

View from the MRT (Metro Trail Transmit) in the heart of Manila

After landing in Manila and suriving the intial culture shock that night, the next morning we took to the streets and did a loop through the city. Our transit was a combination of foot travel, the MRT, tricycles, and of course jeepneys.

Left: Tricycles Right: Jeepeny

In the midst of Manila it was hot, humid, suffocating. After less than half a day we were ready to leave the humungous metropolitan and go to a less urban area.

Baclaran market

Davao City

Davao City is the captiol of the southern most island in the Philippines, Mindinau. It's tropical rainforest climate is home to wonderful fruits, many of which don't grow anywhere else in the world!

We took a short plane ride from Manila to Davao. Compared to Manila, Davao was much more open, less crowded, and had less pollution.

Davao City, home of the infamous durian

Davao is nicknamed "The durian capitol of the Philippines". The common saying is that durian "smells like hell, tastes like heaven". It's smell has been compared to rotting meat, turpentine, gym socks, and rotten eggs. This is no joke. The fruit can be smelled from many yards away and is not allowed in most hotels! Several of my traveling partners gagged upon trying it (I didn't mind it).

It is advised to wear gloves while eating durian to avoid smelling like rotting eggs

Samal Island

The Island Garden City of Samal, a short outrigger ride away from Davao City, is home to many lovely beaches and small resorts.
