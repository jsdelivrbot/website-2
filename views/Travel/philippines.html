I visited the Philippines and Hong Kong from Dec. 29th to January 12th 2018

Manila

Manila, the capitol and chief city of the Philippines, is the center of the country's economic, political, social, and cultural activity. The official language is Taglogog and the most popular sport is basketball. It also has the most dense city proper in the world.

View from the MRT (Metro Rail Transmit) in the heart of Manila

The morning after landing in Manila we took to the streets and did a loop through the city. Our transit was a combination of foot travel, MRT rides, tricycles, and of course jeepneys.

Left: Tricycles Right: Jeepeny

In Manila it was hot, humid and suffocating. The markets stretched for miles and one could easily get lost in the many indoor malls. After less than half a day in those crowded streets we were ready to leave the metropolis and go to a different island.

Baclaran market

Davao City

Davao City is the captiol of the southern most island in the Philippines, Mindinau. It's tropical rainforest climate is home to wonderful fruits, many of which don't grow anywhere else in the world.

Davao City, home of the infamous durian

Davao is nicknamed "The durian capitol of the Philippines". The common saying is that durian "smells like hell, tastes like heaven". It's smell has been compared to rotting meat, turpentine, gym socks, and rotten eggs. This is no joke. The fruit can be smelled from many yards away and is not allowed in most hotels! Several of my traveling partners gagged upon trying it.

It is advised to wear gloves while eating durian to avoid smelling like rotten eggs

Mindinau

We drove across Mindinau Island from Davao City to Lake Sebu. The roads on this trek were often unpaved and varied in quality. Most of the other vehicles accompying us on the road were not cars, but motorcycles and improvised tricycles. The western part of Mindinau is considered a high risk area for foreign travelers.

Lake Sebu

Located in the tropical rainforests of southwestern Mindinau, Lake Sebu is a natural, fresh water lake. Many believe that it is home to the freshest tilapia in the Philippines due to its high elevation.

Left: Rain forest Right: Rice paddies

We took a ride on the highest zipline in southeast asia.

On our way back, we couldn't resist stopping for some balut. Balut is a developing bird embryo (usually a duck) anywhere between 14 and 21 days old. It is boiled and then eaten directly from the shell. Balut is commonly sold as street food throughout the Philippines.

IloIlo City

IloIlo City is located on Panay island, an island in central Philippines. It has one of the fastest growing economies in the Philippines and has many churches with Spanish baroque architecture.

Guimaras Island

Guimaras island is a short outrigger trip from IloIlo. Locals say that it has the best mangos in the Philippines. It also has many beautiful beaches and is largely without many commerical buildings.

Mambukal

Mambukal is a resort located on Nergos Island, an island in central Philippines. It runs along a river with 7 falls and also has natural hot springs from the nearby volcano.

Corregidor

Due to its strategic location in Manila Bay, this island has historically been fortified with coastal artillary to defend against enemy warships. It has been controlled by Spain, the United States, and Japan until the establishment of the Philippines in 1946. During World War 2 Corregidor play an important role during the invasion and liberation of the Philippines.
